Automatic Measurement & Compensation

This equipment provides automatic quality control. It uses plug-n-play robotic arms to transport parts into the measurement station where the dimensions of interest are measured. The measurement data is then compared with the design specs. If the data is beyond the tolerance, the corresponding part will be discarded. If the data is within the tolerance, but warrants a cutting tool offset compensation, an offset is written to the CNC machine over a network connection.


We have partnered with Caron Engineering to create this state of the art device. It combines quality assurance and CNC machine configuration capabilities. The PLC control is responsible for coordinating the robotic arm, CMM device and software components for image processing. The offset is calculated and written back to the CNC machines.

Realtime Remote Data Collection For Quality Assurance

One of our customers is a manufacturer supplying small metal parts for the telecommunication industry. Their operators are responsible for sampling parts on the workshop floor and recording measurement data in paper forms. At the end of the day, another person will enter the data into a computer. The team in the QA room conduct additional measurements and manually type data into Excel sheets on computers. This data will be saved in the computer for record checking purposes.


This practice has several problems: it's labor intensive and error prone; it doesn't prevent workers from entering illegitimate data; data is delayed and not utilized for further analysis.

We identified a newly released bluetooth caliper on the market that can pair with a bluetooth enabled computer pad device or regular computer without any other hardware or software required. This caliper is even cheaper than the popular brands that require wireless adaptor and connection cables. After we converted the Excel sheets/forms to web pages in the MES platform, the QA productivity improved by 30% instantly with realtime quality tracking capability.
